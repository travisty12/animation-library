In Pascal's Triangle, you add together two numbers to find the number beneath them!

Here's a few more rows of the triangle. The terms grow quickly, as you can see.

One of the many cool secrets of the triangle is that the terms in each row 'n' are the coefficients of the terms in the expansion of (x + y)^n.

Every term of Pascal's Triangle can be represented through simple combinatorics, based on its row and column.

This is known as the "n choose k" writing of combinations; the symbolic representation of the number of ways to make a group of k items out of a set of n total items.
